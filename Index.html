Why did price fall while Long volume exceeded Short?
1 — Buy volume ≠ buying pressure
Buy TXs averaged $228 each while Sell TXs averaged $87. The wallet accumulated in large chunks and distributed in many small ones. This is a distribution pattern — it adds sell-side supply to the market continuously, which suppresses price regardless of the "Buy" label on the method.
2 — The wallet is delta-neutral by design
Buy and Sell are nearly equal in dollar terms ($270,959 vs $265,385 — a gap of just $5,574 on $536K). The wallet was cycling inventory, not accumulating a directional position. It profits from spread and fees, not from price appreciation.
3 — Short positions confirm bearish hedging
$167K in short activity ran simultaneously with the long book. Open Short and Close Short are nearly balanced ($84,128 vs $83,161), meaning the wallet maintained a live short hedge throughout the session. Net directional exposure was close to zero.
4 — Volume collapsed after 15:00 UTC
The hourly breakdown shows volume dropped from $445K at 09:00 to under $7K by 16:00. When the market maker stepped back, liquidity dried up. Without a buyer of last resort absorbing sell pressure, price fell freely into the 17:00 wick.
5 — This wallet was not the whole market
Total on-chart volume was $1.51M. This wallet represents part of that flow. Other participants were net sellers throughout the session, and the market maker's withdrawal at 16:00 exposed the underlying bearish order flow that had been masked by its activity.
